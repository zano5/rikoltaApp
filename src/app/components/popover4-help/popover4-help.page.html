Every family is different, and not everyone wants the same type of funeral.
Funeral practices are influenced by religious and cultural traditions, costs, and personal preferences.

These factors help determine whether the funeral will be elaborate or simple,
public or private, religious or secular, and where it will be held.

They also influence whether the body will be present at the funeral, if there will be a viewing or visitation,
and, if so, whether the casket will be open or closed and whether the remains will be buried or cremated.
